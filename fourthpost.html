These statistics about average American expenses and wages are shocking...

"The cost of living is going up and the chance of living is going down." - Flip Wilson

Posted by Team 38 on April 28, 2023

Monthly Expense Statistics (First Republic)

1) A single American family on average spends over $5000 a month.

2) Housing takes the largest percentage of monthly spending at 35%, followed by transportation with 16%.

3) Insurance is the third biggest expense, averaging 12% of expenses.

4) All food expenses average around 12% of total spending.

5) Healthcare makes up around 8% of total spending, which is close to $431 per month.

Minimum Wage Statistics (FinMasters)

1) The United States minimum wage was the highest during 1968 (adjusted for 2022 dollars).

2) To compensate for price increases, data shows that the minimum wage should be increased to $10.01.

3) High school graduates make up over one-third of minimum wage workers:

"According to the Bureau of Labour Statistics, 34.4% of minimum wage workers are high school graduates, while only 0.4% of those with a professional degree are minimum wage workers. Workers who went to college but have no degree make up 27.2% of minimum wage workers, a higher percentage than those with 1 to 3 years of high school (10.3%)" (FinMasters).

4) Women make up a majority of minimum wage workers at 63.5%.

5) The United States has a lower minimum wage (in U.S. dollars) compared to Germany, France, Australia, United Kingdom, and Canada.

Information by First Republic & FinMasters
