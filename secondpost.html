How raising the federal minimum wage specifically benefits the economy of the United States

"We should raise the minimum wage so that no one who works full time has to live in poverty" - Barack Obama

Posted by Gavin Hutchings on April 28, 2023

Raising the federal minimum wage in the United States has a cluster of positive effects, especially in conjunction when discussing the economy. Two of the most impactful outcomes of doing so at a federal level are within two branches of socioeconomic research. Raising wages has effects on the financial system of the government such as increased consumer spending. Another way is inside the social employment structure of the United States which leads to low numbers of unemployment.

Planting the Seed

As an American, there are a lot of freedoms and your wage absolutely should not restrict an individual from reaching their fullest. The minimum wage now is $7.25/hour. Tipped workers make $2.13/hour. These numbers are crucially low and have not been raised since July of 2009. Inflation has worsened since and nobody has benefitted from this. By raising the federal minimum wage, it contributes to the economy through increased consumer spending. When an individual has more money, they’re willing to spend more. This benefits widespread businesses domestically across the United States but also internationally. A win-win situation, symbiotically speaking.

Low Unemployment Among Others

Viewing the current unemployment rate is a great way to manage socio economic issues in the United States. Through this course of action, raising the federal minimum wage impacts workers in work environments. Worker productivity increases which leads to a more positive mental health status, workers want to work more – boosting the economy overall. Another fact brought upon raising the wage is turnover rates. Although these are directly intertwined, both are equally important. Turnovers of workers in businesses would be far lower, leaving less of a headache. With so many positive effects of raising the federal minimum wage, the government of the United States should reconsider and put their citizens first for once.
